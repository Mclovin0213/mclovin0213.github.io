My Top 10 Films of 2019

10. Us

Us is a 2019 horror film written and directed by Jordan Peele. The film follows the story of Adelaide Wilson and her family, who are terrorized by a group of doppelgängers of themselves. The film explores themes of identity, family, and the shadow self. Us received critical acclaim for its direction, screenplay, and performances, particularly from Lupita Nyong'o in the lead role.
9. Villains

Villains is a 2019 dark comedy thriller film directed by Dan Berk and Robert Olsen. The film follows the story of a pair of amateur criminals who break into a house and discover a young girl being held captive in the basement. The film stars Bill Skarsgård, Maika Monroe, Jeffrey Donovan, and Kyra Sedgwick. Villains received positive reviews for its dark humor, performances, and direction.
8. Once Upon a Time in Hollywood

Once Upon a Time in Hollywood is a 2019 comedy-drama film written and directed by Quentin Tarantino. The film stars Leonardo DiCaprio and Brad Pitt as a fading Hollywood actor and his stunt double, respectively, as they try to navigate the changing film industry and the Manson Family murders in 1969 Los Angeles. The film received critical acclaim for its direction, screenplay, and performances, and was nominated for multiple awards, including ten Oscars.
7. The Peanut Butter Falcon

A young man with Down syndrome runs away from his care home to become a professional wrestler, with the help of a small-time outlaw who becomes his coach.
6. Knives Out

A detective investigates the death of a patriarch of an eccentric, combative family, after his 85th birthday party.
5. The Lighthouse

The hypnotic and hallucinatory tale of two lighthouse keepers on a remote and mysterious New England island in the 1890s.
4. Monos

A group of teenage commandos who have been trained to guard an American hostage on a remote mountain in Colombia must confront their own humanity when they come into contact with the hostage.
3. Marriage Story

A stage director and his actor wife struggle through a grueling, coast-to-coast divorce that pushes them to their personal and creative extremes.
2. Parasite

Greed and class discrimination threaten the newly formed symbiotic relationship between the wealthy Park family and the destitute Kim clan.
1. 1917

Two young British soldiers during the First World War are given an impossible mission: deliver a message deep in enemy territory that will stop 1,600 men, and one of the soldier's brothers, from walking straight into a deadly trap.
