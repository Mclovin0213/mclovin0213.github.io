My Top 10 Animated Films

10. The Spongebob Squarepants Movie

It's a movie about a sponge who lives in a pineapple under the sea. He's got a pet snail, a best friend named Patrick, and a talking dog named Sandy. He works at a fast food restaurant called the Krusty Krab, and he's got a nemesis named Plankton. He's got a lot going on, but he's a good guy. He's a good guy.
9. Fantastic Planet

It's a French animated science fiction film directed by René Laloux and written by Laloux and Roland Topor. The film depicts a future in which humans are a feral race, while a species of giant humanoid aliens called Draags are highly intelligent and live a peaceful life. The Draags' treatment of humans is akin to humans' treatment of animals, with humans kept as pets and experimented on. The film explores themes of oppression, the relationship between humans and nature, and the nature of consciousness.
8. The Lego Movie

The Lego Movie is a 2014 computer-animated adventure comedy film directed and written by Phil Lord and Christopher Miller, from a story by Lord, Miller and Dan and Kevin Hageman. The film follows an ordinary Lego minifigure named Emmet who is mistakenly identified as the most extraordinary person and the key to saving the Lego universe. Emmet and his allies must stop the evil Lord Business from taking over the world. The film was praised for its animation, voice acting, humor, and original story, as well as its clever use of Lego bricks.
7. Inside Out

Inside Out is a Pixar film about a young girl named Riley who moves to a new city with her family. The story is told from the perspective of her emotions: Joy, Sadness, Anger, Fear, and Disgust. When Joy and Sadness get lost in Riley's mind, they have to work together to find their way back to the control center and help Riley cope with her new life.
6. Kiki's Delivery Service

Kiki's Delivery Service is a Studio Ghibli film about a young witch named Kiki who moves to a new city to start her own delivery service. Along the way, she meets new friends and overcomes various obstacles, including losing her ability to fly on her broomstick. The film is known for its charming characters and beautiful animation.
5. My Life as a Zucchini

My Life as a Zucchini is a stop-motion animated film about a young boy named Zucchini who is sent to live in an orphanage after his mother's death. There, he befriends the other children and learns to overcome his difficult past. The film deals with themes of loss, trauma, and resilience in a sensitive and heartfelt way.
4. Spider-Man: Spider-verse

Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse is an animated superhero film that follows the story of Miles Morales, a teenager who gains spider-like abilities and becomes the new Spider-Man. He teams up with other Spider-People from different dimensions to stop a threat that endangers all of reality. The film won critical acclaim for its unique animation style and engaging storyline.
3. Ratatouille

Ratatouille is a Pixar film about a rat named Remy who dreams of becoming a chef. He forms an unlikely partnership with a human kitchen worker named Linguini and together, they create a series of culinary masterpieces that impress the toughest food critic in Paris. The film is known for its heartwarming story and stunning animation.
2. My Neighbor Totoro

My Neighbor Totoro is a Studio Ghibli film about two sisters, Satsuki and Mei, who move to the countryside with their father while their mother recovers from an illness. There, they meet a forest spirit named Totoro and embark on magical adventures together. The film is beloved for its charming characters, whimsical story, and stunning animation.
1. A Town Called Panic

A Town Called Panic is a 2009 stop-motion animated adventure comedy film directed by Stéphane Aubier and Vincent Patar. The film follows the misadventures of Cowboy, Indian and Horse, three plastic toys who share a rambling house in a rural town that never fails to attract the weirdest events. When Cowboy and Indian's plan to surprise Horse with a homemade barbecue pit goes awry, the trio embarks on a series of increasingly surreal and hilarious escapades involving sea monsters, ninja warriors, and an underwater parallel universe. The film received critical acclaim for its imaginative and absurd humor and has become a cult classic.
