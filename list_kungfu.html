My Top 10 Martial Arts/Kung-Fu Films

10. Kung Fu Hustle

In Shanghai, China in the 1940s, a wannabe gangster aspires to join the notorious "Axe Gang" while residents of a housing complex exhibit extraordinary powers in defending their turf.
9. Master of the Flying Guillotine

A one-armed martial arts master is targeted by the vengeful flying guillotine gang, who have already killed his two disciples.
8. Police Story 2

The Hong Kong super-cop must stop a group of blackmailing bombers, while the villains of Police Story (1985) are out for revenge.
7. Kill Bill Vol 2

Kill Bill Vol 2 is the second installment of Quentin Tarantino's epic revenge saga. Uma Thurman returns as "The Bride," a former assassin seeking revenge against the people who tried to kill her. The film is a masterful blend of action, drama, and comedy, with standout performances from Thurman and David Carradine.
6. Return to the 36th Chamber

Return to the 36th Chamber is a classic kung fu movie that tells the story of a young man who learns kung fu to save his town from an evil gang. The film features incredible martial arts sequences and a charming performance from Gordon Liu as the hero. Return to the 36th Chamber is a must-see for fans of the genre.
5. Enter the Dragon

Enter the Dragon is a classic martial arts film starring Bruce Lee as a man who is recruited by a British intelligence agency to infiltrate a crime lord's operation. The film features incredible fight sequences and Lee's iconic performance, and it remains a beloved classic of the genre.
4. Matrix

The Matrix is a science fiction action film that tells the story of a computer hacker named Neo, who discovers that his entire life has been a virtual reality simulation created by machines. He is then recruited by a group of rebels to help overthrow the machines and free humanity.
3. Hero

Hero is a wuxia film directed by Zhang Yimou and stars Jet Li. The story is set in ancient China and follows a warrior named Nameless who is summoned by the King of Qin to tell the story of how he defeated three assassins who were trying to kill the king. The film uses stunning visuals and martial arts choreography to tell a complex and emotional story about loyalty and sacrifice.
2. Kill Bill Vol 1

Kill Bill Vol 1 is a 2003 martial arts film written and directed by Quentin Tarantino. It stars Uma Thurman as the Bride, a former member of a team of assassins who seeks revenge on her ex-colleagues who betrayed her and tried to kill her on her wedding day.
1. Police Story

Police Story is a 1985 Hong Kong action film directed by and starring Jackie Chan as a police detective named Chan Ka-kui. The film is known for its innovative and dangerous stunt work and has been considered a classic in the action genre.
